Question 1: Household Fan Flow Rates

Question:

A household fan with a blade diameter of $0.75$ m draws in air at $98$ kPa and $295$ K. It discharges the air at $105$ kPa and $296$ K with an exit velocity of $1.5$ m/s. Determine:
a) The mass flow rate ($\dot{m}$) of the air in kg/s.
b) The inlet velocity ($V_1$) of the air.
c) The outgoing volume flow rate ($\dot{V}_2$) in m³/s.

Final Answer:

a) Mass flow rate $\dot{m} \approx 0.819$ kg/s.
b) Inlet velocity $V_1 \approx 1.60$ m/s.
c) Outgoing volume flow rate $\dot{V}_2 \approx 0.663$ m³/s.

Full Solution:

1. Identify States & Properties:

  • Inlet (1): $P_1 = 98$ kPa, $T_1 = 295$ K.
  • Outlet (2): $P_2 = 105$ kPa, $T_2 = 296$ K, $V_2 = 1.5$ m/s.
  • Fan diameter $D = 0.75$ m. Area $A = \pi D^2 / 4 = \pi (0.75)^2 / 4 \approx 0.4418$ m². Assume $A_1 = A_2 = A$.
  • Air ideal gas, $R = 0.287$ kJ/kg-K (Table A.5).

2. Calculate Exit Specific Volume ($v_2$):

$$ v_2 = \frac{R T_2}{P_2} = \frac{0.287 \frac{\text{kJ}}{\text{kg-K}} \times 296 \text{ K}}{105 \text{ kPa}} = \frac{84.952}{105} = 0.80907 \frac{\text{m³}}{\text{kg}} $$

3. Calculate Outgoing Volume Flow Rate ($\dot{V}_2$) - Part (c):

$$ \dot{V}_2 = A_2 V_2 = 0.4418 \text{ m²} \times 1.5 \frac{\text{m}}{\text{s}} = 0.6627 \frac{\text{m³}}{\text{s}} \approx 0.663 \frac{\text{m³}}{\text{s}} $$

4. Calculate Mass Flow Rate ($\dot{m}$) - Part (a):

$$ \dot{m} = \frac{\dot{V}_2}{v_2} = \frac{0.6627 \text{ m³/s}}{0.80907 \text{ m³/kg}} = 0.81908 \frac{\text{kg}}{\text{s}} \approx 0.819 \frac{\text{kg}}{\text{s}} $$

5. Calculate Inlet Specific Volume ($v_1$):

$$ v_1 = \frac{R T_1}{P_1} = \frac{0.287 \frac{\text{kJ}}{\text{kg-K}} \times 295 \text{ K}}{98 \text{ kPa}} = \frac{84.665}{98} = 0.8639 \frac{\text{m³}}{\text{kg}} $$

6. Calculate Inlet Velocity ($V_1$) - Part (b):

Use mass continuity: $\dot{m} = \frac{A_1 V_1}{v_1} \implies V_1 = \frac{\dot{m} v_1}{A_1}$

$$ V_1 = \frac{0.81908 \frac{\text{kg}}{\text{s}} \times 0.8639 \frac{\text{m³}}{\text{kg}}}{0.4418 \text{ m²}} = \frac{0.7076}{0.4418} = 1.6016 \frac{\text{m}}{\text{s}} \approx 1.60 \frac{\text{m}}{\text{s}} $$

Table Values Used Summary: A.5 (R for Air).

Question 2: Throttling Water to Flash Evaporator

Question:

Liquid water at $180$°C and $2000$ kPa is passed through a throttling device (like a valve) into a flash evaporator chamber maintained at a pressure of $500$ kPa. Neglecting any changes in kinetic energy, determine the fraction of liquid and vapor (by mass) in the chamber after the throttling process.

Final Answer:
  • Liquid fraction ($1-x_2$) $\approx 94.1\%$
  • Vapor fraction ($x_2$) $\approx 5.9\%$
Full Solution:

1. Process: Throttling (constant enthalpy). $h_2 = h_1$. Neglect KE/PE.

2. State 1: $T_1=180$°C, $P_1=2000$ kPa. This is compressed liquid ($P_1 > P_{sat}(180°C)$).

From Table B.1.4 (Compressed Liquid Water) at $P=2$ MPa, $T=180$°C:

  • $h_1 = 763.71$ kJ/kg.

(Approximation using $h_f(180°C)$ from Table B.1.1 gives $763.21$ kJ/kg, using the compressed liquid table is more accurate).

3. State 2: $P_2 = 500$ kPa, $h_2 = h_1 = 763.71$ kJ/kg.

Check saturation properties at $P_2 = 500$ kPa (Table B.1.2):

  • $h_f = 640.21$ kJ/kg
  • $h_g = 2748.67$ kJ/kg
  • $h_{fg} = 2108.47$ kJ/kg

Since $h_f < h_2 < h_g$, state 2 is a saturated liquid-vapor mixture.

4. Calculate Quality ($x_2$) at State 2:

$$ h_2 = h_f + x_2 h_{fg} $$ $$ 763.71 = 640.21 + x_2 \times 2108.47 $$ $$ x_2 \times 2108.47 = 763.71 - 640.21 = 123.50 $$ $$ x_2 = \frac{123.50}{2108.47} = 0.058575 \approx 0.0586 $$

5. Determine Fractions:

  • Vapor fraction (by mass) = $x_2 \approx 0.0586$ or $5.86\%$.
  • Liquid fraction (by mass) = $1 - x_2 \approx 1 - 0.0586 = 0.9414$ or $94.14\%$.

Table Values Used Summary: B.1.4 (Compressed Water), B.1.2 (Saturated Water).

Question 3: Nitrogen Nozzle Exit Velocity

Question:

Nitrogen gas (N₂) enters a convergent nozzle at $200$ kPa and $400$ K with negligible inlet velocity. It exits the nozzle at $100$ kPa and $300$ K. Assuming the nozzle is insulated (adiabatic), find the exit velocity ($V_2$).

Final Answer:

Exit velocity $V_2 \approx 456.4$ m/s.

Full Solution:

1. Process: Steady flow through an adiabatic nozzle. No work done.

2. States:

  • Inlet (1): $P_1=200$ kPa, $T_1=400$ K, $V_1 \approx 0$.
  • Outlet (2): $P_2=100$ kPa, $T_2=300$ K, $V_2 = ?$.

3. Energy Balance (SFEE):

$$ h_1 + \frac{V_1^2}{2} = h_2 + \frac{V_2^2}{2} $$

With $V_1 \approx 0$:

$$ \frac{V_2^2}{2} = h_1 - h_2 $$ $$ V_2 = \sqrt{2 (h_1 - h_2)} $$

4. Get Enthalpy Values (Table A.8 for N₂):

  • $h_1 (\text{at } T_1=400 \text{ K}) = 415.81$ kJ/kg
  • $h_2 (\text{at } T_2=300 \text{ K}) = 311.67$ kJ/kg

5. Calculate Exit Velocity ($V_2$):

$$ h_1 - h_2 = 415.81 - 311.67 = 104.14 \frac{\text{kJ}}{\text{kg}} = 104140 \frac{\text{J}}{\text{kg}} $$ $$ V_2 = \sqrt{2 \times 104140 \frac{\text{J}}{\text{kg}}} = \sqrt{208280 \frac{\text{m}^2}{\text{s}^2}} $$ $$ V_2 = 456.38 \frac{\text{m}}{\text{s}} \approx 456.4 \frac{\text{m}}{\text{s}} $$

Table Values Used Summary: A.8 (Nitrogen enthalpies).

Question 4: Jet Engine Diffuser Outlet State

Question:

The front diffuser of a jet engine receives air at $900$ km/h, $-5$°C, and $50$ kPa. The diffuser slows the air down to $80$ m/s relative to the engine before it enters the compressor. The flow area at the diffuser outlet (compressor inlet) is $80\%$ of the diffuser inlet area. Assuming an adiabatic diffuser process and constant specific heats for air, find the temperature and pressure at the compressor inlet (diffuser outlet).

Final Answer:

Compressor Inlet Temperature $T_2 \approx 296.1$ K ($23.0$ °C).
Compressor Inlet Pressure $P_2 \approx 68.0$ kPa.

Full Solution:

1. Identify States & Convert Units:

  • Inlet (1): $V_1 = 900$ km/h $= 250$ m/s, $T_1 = -5$°C $= 268.15$ K, $P_1 = 50$ kPa.
  • Outlet (2): $V_2 = 80$ m/s, $A_2 = 0.80 A_1$. $T_2 = ?, P_2 = ?$
  • Process: Adiabatic diffuser. Steady flow. Air ideal gas, constant $C_p, R, k$.

2. Calculate Exit Temperature ($T_2$):

Use SFEE: $h_1 + V_1^2/2 = h_2 + V_2^2/2$. For constant $C_p$:

$$ C_p(T_2 - T_1) = \frac{V_1^2 - V_2^2}{2} $$ $$ T_2 = T_1 + \frac{V_1^2 - V_2^2}{2 C_p} $$

Use $C_p = 1.004$ kJ/kg-K $= 1004$ J/kg-K (Table A.5).

$$ T_2 = 268.15 \text{ K} + \frac{(250)^2 - (80)^2}{2 \times 1004} = 268.15 + \frac{62500 - 6400}{2008} $$ $$ T_2 = 268.15 + \frac{56100}{2008} = 268.15 + 27.938 = 296.088 \text{ K} \approx 296.1 \text{ K} $$

3. Calculate Exit Pressure ($P_2$):

Use Mass Continuity: $\dot{m}_1 = \dot{m}_2 \implies \rho_1 A_1 V_1 = \rho_2 A_2 V_2$.

For ideal gas, $\rho = P/(RT)$.

$$ \frac{P_1}{R T_1} A_1 V_1 = \frac{P_2}{R T_2} A_2 V_2 $$

Cancel R and rearrange for $P_2$:

$$ P_2 = P_1 \frac{A_1}{A_2} \frac{V_1}{V_2} \frac{T_2}{T_1} $$

Given $A_2 = 0.8 A_1 \implies A_1/A_2 = 1/0.8 = 1.25$.

$$ P_2 = 50 \text{ kPa} \times (1.25) \times (\frac{250 \text{ m/s}}{80 \text{ m/s}}) \times (\frac{296.088 \text{ K}}{268.15 \text{ K}}) $$ $$ P_2 = 50 \times 1.25 \times 3.125 \times 1.10418 $$ $$ P_2 = 62.5 \times 3.125 \times 1.10418 = 195.31 \times 1.10418 = 215.66 \text{ kPa} \approx 215.7 \text{ kPa} $$

Table Values Used Summary: A.5 (Cp, R for Air).

Question 5: Wind Turbine Power

Question:

A wind turbine with a rotor diameter of $20$ m extracts $40\%$ of the kinetic energy from the incoming wind as shaft work. On a particular day, the air temperature is $20$°C, the air pressure is assumed to be standard atmospheric ($101.325$ kPa), and the wind speed is $35$ km/h. Determine the power produced by the turbine.

Final Answer:

The power produced is approximately $69.5$ kW.

Full Solution:

1. Parameters:

  • Diameter $D = 20$ m
  • Efficiency $\eta = 40\% = 0.40$
  • Air Temperature $T = 20$°C $= 293.15$ K
  • Air Pressure $P = 101.325$ kPa
  • Wind Speed $V = 35$ km/h $= 35 \times 1000 / 3600 = 9.722$ m/s

2. Calculate Air Density ($\rho$): Ideal gas law.

$$ \rho = \frac{P}{R T} = \frac{101.325 \text{ kPa}}{(0.287 \frac{\text{kJ}}{\text{kg-K}}) (293.15 \text{ K})} = \frac{101.325}{84.134} = 1.2043 \frac{\text{kg}}{\text{m³}} $$

3. Calculate Rotor Area (A):

$$ A = \frac{\pi D^2}{4} = \frac{\pi (20 \text{ m})^2}{4} = 100\pi \approx 314.16 \text{ m²} $$

4. Calculate Incoming Kinetic Energy Rate ($\dot{KE}_{in}$):

This is the power available in the wind passing through the rotor area.

$$ \dot{KE}_{in} = \frac{1}{2} \dot{m} V^2 = \frac{1}{2} (\rho A V) V^2 = \frac{1}{2} \rho A V^3 $$ $$ \dot{KE}_{in} = \frac{1}{2} (1.2043 \frac{\text{kg}}{\text{m³}}) (314.16 \text{ m²}) (9.722 \frac{\text{m}}{\text{s}})^3 $$ $$ \dot{KE}_{in} = 0.5 \times 1.2043 \times 314.16 \times 918.9 = 173834 \text{ W} = 173.8 \text{ kW} $$

5. Calculate Power Produced ($\dot{W}$):

The turbine extracts 40% of this incoming power.

$$ \dot{W} = \eta \times \dot{KE}_{in} = 0.40 \times 173.8 \text{ kW} = 69.52 \text{ kW} \approx 69.5 \text{ kW} $$

Table Values Used Summary: A.5 (R for Air).

Question 6: R-410A Compressor and Cooler

Question:

An adiabatic compressor takes in R-410A as saturated vapor at $400$ kPa. It compresses the refrigerant to $2000$ kPa and $60$°C. Subsequently, the R-410A passes through a cooler (heat exchanger) where it is cooled at constant pressure until it becomes saturated liquid at $2000$ kPa. Determine:
a) The specific work input required by the compressor ($w_c$).
b) The specific heat transfer removed in the cooler ($q_{cool}$).

Final Answer:

a) Specific compressor work $w_c \approx 75.5$ kJ/kg.
b) Specific heat transfer in cooler $q_{cool} \approx -237.2$ kJ/kg.

Full Solution:

1. Identify States:

  • State 1 (Compressor Inlet): $P_1 = 400$ kPa, $x_1 = 1$ (sat. vapor).
  • State 2 (Compressor Outlet / Cooler Inlet): $P_2 = 2000$ kPa, $T_2 = 60$°C.
  • State 3 (Cooler Outlet): $P_3 = 2000$ kPa, $x_3 = 0$ (sat. liquid).

2. Find Enthalpies:

  • State 1: From Table B.4.2 (Superheated - reading sat vapor line): $h_1 = h_g(400 \text{ kPa}) = 271.90$ kJ/kg.
  • State 2: From Table B.4.2 (Superheated): $h_2(2000 \text{ kPa}, 60°C) = 347.40$ kJ/kg.
  • State 3: Saturated liquid at $P_3=2000$ kPa. $T_{sat}(2000 \text{ kPa}) = 32.31$°C. Need $h_f$ at this state. Interpolate in Table B.4.1 between $T=30$°C ($h_f=106.14$) and $T=35$°C ($h_f=114.95$):
    $h_3 = 106.14 + \frac{32.31 - 30}{35 - 30} \times (114.95 - 106.14) = 106.14 + 0.462 \times 8.81 = 106.14 + 4.07 = 110.21$ kJ/kg.

3. Calculate Compressor Work ($w_c$): SFEE for adiabatic compressor ($Q=0, \Delta KE=\Delta PE=0$): $\dot{W}_{in}/\dot{m} = h_2 - h_1$.

$$ w_c = h_2 - h_1 = 347.40 - 271.90 = 75.5 \frac{\text{kJ}}{\text{kg}} $$

4. Calculate Cooler Heat Transfer ($q_{cool}$): SFEE for cooler ($W=0, \Delta KE=\Delta PE=0$): $\dot{Q}_{out}/\dot{m} = h_2 - h_3$. $q_{cool} = h_3 - h_2$.

$$ q_{cool} = h_3 - h_2 = 110.21 - 347.40 = -237.19 \frac{\text{kJ}}{\text{kg}} \approx -237.2 \frac{\text{kJ}}{\text{kg}} $$

(Negative sign indicates heat is removed).

Table Values Used Summary: B.4.1, B.4.2 (R-410A).

Question 7: Water Boiler Heat Transfer

Question:

A boiler section operating at a constant pressure of $4000$ kPa takes in $3$ kg/s of saturated liquid water and boils it completely to saturated vapor. Find the total rate of heat transfer required in this process. Represent the process on P-v and T-v diagrams.

Final Answer:

The rate of heat transfer is $\dot{Q} \approx 5142$ kW (or $5.14$ MW).

Full Solution:

1. Process: Constant pressure heating ($P=4000$ kPa) from sat. liquid to sat. vapor.

2. States:

  • State 1: Sat. liquid ($x_1=0$) at $P_1 = 4000$ kPa.
  • State 2: Sat. vapor ($x_2=1$) at $P_2 = 4000$ kPa.
  • Mass flow $\dot{m} = 3$ kg/s.

3. Energy Balance (Boiler Section): SFEE with $W=0, \Delta KE=0, \Delta PE=0$.

$$ \dot{Q} = \dot{m} (h_2 - h_1) = \dot{m} h_{fg} $$

4. Find Enthalpy of Vaporization ($h_{fg}$ at 4000 kPa):

From Table B.1.2:

  • $h_{fg} = 1714.09$ kJ/kg. (Also $T_{sat}=250.40$°C).

5. Calculate Heat Transfer Rate ($\dot{Q}$):

$$ \dot{Q} = 3 \frac{\text{kg}}{\text{s}} \times 1714.09 \frac{\text{kJ}}{\text{kg}} = 5142.27 \frac{\text{kJ}}{\text{s}} = 5142.27 \text{ kW} \approx 5142 \text{ kW} $$

6. Diagrams:

  • P-v Diagram: Draw saturation dome. Process is a horizontal line at $P = 4000$ kPa from $v_f$ to $v_g$.
  • T-v Diagram: Draw saturation dome. Process is a horizontal line at $T = T_{sat} = 250.40$°C from $v_f$ to $v_g$.

Table Values Used Summary: B.1.2 (Water).

Question 8: Steam Pipe Heat Loss & Inlet Temp

Question:

A steam pipe in a $300$ m tall building transports superheated steam. At the ground level (inlet), the pressure is $200$ kPa. At the top floor (outlet), the pressure is $125$ kPa. The heat loss from the steam as it flows through the pipe is $110$ kJ/kg. What should the inlet temperature ($T_1$) be to ensure that no condensation occurs (i.e., the steam at the outlet is saturated vapor)? Neglect kinetic energy changes.

Final Answer:

The inlet temperature should be $T_1 \approx 164.5$ °C.

Full Solution:

1. Process: Steady flow in pipe with heat loss and elevation change.

2. States:

  • Inlet (1): $P_1 = 200$ kPa, $z_1 = 0$ m, $T_1 = ?$.
  • Outlet (2): $P_2 = 125$ kPa, $z_2 = 300$ m, State: sat. vapor ($x_2 = 1$).

3. Given: Heat loss $q_{out} = 110$ kJ/kg $\implies q_{in} = -110$ kJ/kg. $\Delta KE \approx 0$.

4. Find State 2 Properties: $P_2 = 125$ kPa, $x_2 = 1$. From Table B.1.2:

  • $h_2 = h_g = 2685.35$ kJ/kg.

5. Apply Steady Flow Energy Equation (SFEE):

$$ q_{in} + h_1 + \frac{V_1^2}{2} + gz_1 = h_2 + \frac{V_2^2}{2} + gz_2 + w $$

With $q_{in} = -110$, $w=0$, $\Delta KE \approx 0$, $z_1=0$:

$$ -110 + h_1 = h_2 + gz_2 $$ $$ h_1 = h_2 + gz_2 + 110 $$

6. Calculate Potential Energy Term ($gz_2$):

$$ gz_2 = (9.80665 \frac{\text{m}}{\text{s²}}) (300 \text{ m}) = 2941.995 \frac{\text{J}}{\text{kg}} = 2.942 \frac{\text{kJ}}{\text{kg}} $$

7. Calculate Inlet Enthalpy ($h_1$):

$$ h_1 = 2685.35 + 2.942 + 110 = 2798.29 \frac{\text{kJ}}{\text{kg}} $$

8. Find Inlet Temperature ($T_1$):

State 1: $P_1 = 200$ kPa, $h_1 = 2798.29$ kJ/kg. Check saturation at 200 kPa (Table B.1.2): $h_g = 2706.63$ kJ/kg. Since $h_1 > h_g$, state 1 is superheated.

Interpolate in Table B.1.3 at $P=200$ kPa between $T=150$°C ($h=2768.80$) and $T=200$°C ($h=2870.46$):

$$ T_1 = 150 \text{°C} + \frac{2798.29 - 2768.80}{2870.46 - 2768.80} \times (200 - 150) \text{°C} $$ $$ T_1 = 150 + \frac{29.49}{101.66} \times 50 = 150 + 0.29008 \times 50 $$ $$ T_1 = 150 + 14.50 = 164.5 \text{°C} $$

Table Values Used Summary: A.0a (g), B.1.2, B.1.3 (Water).

Question 9: Radiator Flow Rates

Question:

An automotive radiator uses glycol entering at $95$°C and leaving at $55$°C. Air flows across the radiator, entering at $20$°C and leaving at $25$°C. The radiator transfers heat at a rate of $25$ kW. Assuming constant specific heats and an air pressure of $100$ kPa, find:
a) The mass flow rate of the glycol ($\dot{m}_g$).
b) The volume flow rate of the air at the inlet ($\dot{V}_{a1}$).

Final Answer:

a) Mass flow rate of glycol $\dot{m}_g \approx 0.272$ kg/s.
b) Volume flow rate of air $\dot{V}_{a1} \approx 4.19$ m³/s.

Full Solution:

1. Energy Balance: Heat lost by glycol = Heat gained by air = $\dot{Q} = 25$ kW.

2. Glycol Mass Flow Rate ($\dot{m}_g$):

$\dot{Q} = \dot{m}_g C_{p,g} (T_{g,in} - T_{g,out})$. Use $C_{p,g} \approx 2.3$ kJ/kg-K (Table A.4).

$$ 25 \text{ kW} = \dot{m}_g \times 2.3 \frac{\text{kJ}}{\text{kg-K}} \times (95 - 55) \text{ K} $$ $$ 25 = \dot{m}_g \times 2.3 \times 40 = \dot{m}_g \times 92 $$ $$ \dot{m}_g = \frac{25}{92} = 0.2717 \frac{\text{kg}}{\text{s}} \approx 0.272 \frac{\text{kg}}{\text{s}} $$

3. Air Mass Flow Rate ($\dot{m}_a$):

$\dot{Q} = \dot{m}_a C_{p,a} (T_{a,out} - T_{a,in})$. Use $C_{p,a} = 1.004$ kJ/kg-K (Table A.5).

$$ 25 \text{ kW} = \dot{m}_a \times 1.004 \frac{\text{kJ}}{\text{kg-K}} \times (25 - 20) \text{ K} $$ $$ 25 = \dot{m}_a \times 1.004 \times 5 = \dot{m}_a \times 5.02 $$ $$ \dot{m}_a = \frac{25}{5.02} = 4.980 \frac{\text{kg}}{\text{s}} $$

4. Air Inlet Specific Volume ($v_{a1}$):

Assume ideal gas. $T_{a1} = 20$°C $= 293.15$ K. $P_{a1} = 100$ kPa. $R_{air} = 0.287$ kJ/kg-K.

$$ v_{a1} = \frac{R T_{a1}}{P_{a1}} = \frac{0.287 \times 293.15}{100} = \frac{84.134}{100} = 0.84134 \frac{\text{m³}}{\text{kg}} $$

5. Air Inlet Volume Flow Rate ($\dot{V}_{a1}$):

$$ \dot{V}_{a1} = \dot{m}_a v_{a1} = 4.980 \frac{\text{kg}}{\text{s}} \times 0.84134 \frac{\text{m³}}{\text{kg}} = 4.190 \frac{\text{m³}}{\text{s}} $$

Table Values Used Summary: A.4 (Cp Glycol), A.5 (Cp, R for Air).

Question 10: Jet Engine Component Analysis

Question:

A well-insulated modern jet engine operates under the following conditions (referring to standard station numbering):
• Diffuser Inlet (a): $P_a = 75$ kPa, $T_a = 220$ K.
• Compressor Inlet (1): $P_1 = 100$ kPa, $T_1 = 280$ K.
• Compressor Outlet (2): $P_2 = 3450$ kPa, $T_2 = 800$ K.
• Turbine Inlet (3): $P_3 = 3100$ kPa, $T_3 = 1450$ K.
• Turbine Outlet / Nozzle Inlet (4): $P_4 = 425$ kPa, $T_4 = 950$ K.
• Nozzle Outlet (5): $P_5 = 75$ kPa, $T_5 = 620$ K.
Neglect kinetic energy effects except for the nozzle exit velocity. Assume air properties. Determine:
a) Specific work interaction from state 'a' to state 2.
b) Turbine specific work ($w_t$).
c) Nozzle exit velocity ($V_5$).

Final Answer:

a) Specific work input $w_{a \to 2} \approx 541.8$ kJ/kg.
b) Turbine specific work output $w_t \approx 586.0$ kJ/kg.
c) Nozzle exit velocity $V_5 \approx 849.8$ m/s.

Full Solution:

(a) Specific Work from State 'a' to State 2:

This process involves the diffuser (no work) and the compressor (work input). The net specific work interaction is the compressor work $w_c$. Assume negligible KE at compressor inlet (1) and outlet (2).

Apply SFEE to compressor (adiabatic): $w_c = h_2 - h_1$.

Get enthalpies from Table A.7.1:

  • $h_1(T_1=280 \text{ K}) = 280.39$ kJ/kg
  • $h_2(T_2=800 \text{ K}) = 822.20$ kJ/kg
$$ w_c = 822.20 - 280.39 = 541.81 \frac{\text{kJ}}{\text{kg}} $$

This is the work done *by* the compressor *on* the air. The work interaction *from* the surroundings *to* the air is $w_{in} = 541.81$ kJ/kg.

(b) Turbine Specific Work ($w_t$):

Apply SFEE to turbine (adiabatic, negl. KE/PE): $w_t = h_3 - h_4$. (Work done *by* the turbine).

Get enthalpies from Table A.7.1:

  • $h_3(T_3=1450 \text{ K}) = 1575.40$ kJ/kg
  • $h_4(T_4=950 \text{ K}) = 989.44$ kJ/kg
$$ w_t = 1575.40 - 989.44 = 585.96 \frac{\text{kJ}}{\text{kg}} \approx 586.0 \frac{\text{kJ}}{\text{kg}} $$

(c) Nozzle Exit Velocity ($V_5$):

Apply SFEE to nozzle (adiabatic, $w=0$, negl. PE change). Assume $V_4 \approx 0$.

$$ h_4 + \frac{V_4^2}{2} = h_5 + \frac{V_5^2}{2} $$ $$ h_4 \approx h_5 + \frac{V_5^2}{2} $$ $$ \frac{V_5^2}{2} = h_4 - h_5 $$

Get enthalpy from Table A.7.1:

  • $h_5(T_5=620 \text{ K}) = 628.38$ kJ/kg

Question 11: Bathtub Filling and Draining Time

Question:

An empty bathtub with a closed drain is filled with water from a faucet at a rate of $\dot{m}_{in1} = 10$ kg/min for $10$ minutes. After $10$ minutes, the drain is opened, allowing water to flow out at a rate of $\dot{m}_{out} = 4$ kg/min. Simultaneously, the inlet flow rate from the faucet is reduced to $\dot{m}_{in2} = 2$ kg/min. Determine the total time elapsed from the very beginning until the bathtub becomes empty.

Final Answer:

The total time is $60$ minutes (1 hour).

Full Solution:

1. Phase 1: Filling (0 to 10 minutes):

  • Rate of mass change: $\frac{dm}{dt} = \dot{m}_{in1} = +10$ kg/min.
  • Mass accumulated after 10 minutes: $m_{10} = (\frac{dm}{dt}) \times \Delta t = 10 \frac{\text{kg}}{\text{min}} \times 10 \text{ min} = 100$ kg.

2. Phase 2: Draining and Filling (t > 10 minutes):

  • Inlet rate: $\dot{m}_{in2} = 2$ kg/min.
  • Outlet rate: $\dot{m}_{out} = 4$ kg/min.
  • Net rate of mass change: $\frac{dm}{dt} = \dot{m}_{in2} - \dot{m}_{out} = 2 - 4 = -2$ kg/min. (Mass decreases).

3. Time to Empty during Phase 2:

  • Initial mass for Phase 2: $m = 100$ kg.
  • Rate of decrease: $2$ kg/min.
  • Time required to empty ($\Delta t_2$): $\Delta t_2 = \frac{\text{Initial Mass}}{\text{Rate of Decrease}} = \frac{100 \text{ kg}}{2 \text{ kg/min}} = 50$ minutes.

4. Total Time:

$$ \text{Total time} = \text{Time Phase 1} + \text{Time Phase 2} $$ $$ \text{Total time} = 10 \text{ minutes} + 50 \text{ minutes} = 60 \text{ minutes} $$

Table Values Used: None needed.

Question 12: Tank Filling Heat Transfer

Question:

A rigid tank with a volume of $2$ m³ initially contains air at $100$ kPa and $300$ K. A pipe carrying air at $1000$ kPa and $350$ K is connected to the tank, and the tank is filled slowly until the final pressure inside is $1000$ kPa. The process occurs such that the final temperature inside the tank is $300$ K. Determine the heat transfer ($Q$) needed during this filling process.

Final Answer:

Heat Transfer $Q \approx -2851$ kJ (Heat removed).

Full Solution:

1. System: Air inside the tank (Control Volume). Unsteady filling.

2. States: Air is Ideal Gas. $R=0.287$ kJ/kg-K.

  • Initial (1): $P_1=100$ kPa, $T_1=300$ K, $V=2$ m³.
  • Inlet (i): $P_i=1000$ kPa, $T_i=350$ K.
  • Final (2): $P_2=1000$ kPa, $T_2=300$ K, $V=2$ m³.

3. Mass Calculations: $m = PV / (RT)$.

  • $m_1 = \frac{100 \times 2}{0.287 \times 300} = \frac{200}{86.1} = 2.323$ kg.
  • $m_2 = \frac{1000 \times 2}{0.287 \times 300} = \frac{2000}{86.1} = 23.229$ kg.
  • Mass added: $m_i = m_2 - m_1 = 23.229 - 2.323 = 20.906$ kg.

4. Get Properties (Table A.7.1):

  • $u_1 = u(300 \text{ K}) = 214.36$ kJ/kg
  • $h_i = h(350 \text{ K}) \approx 350.78$ kJ/kg (Interpolated: $340.70 + 0.5(360.86-340.70)$)
  • $u_2 = u(300 \text{ K}) = 214.36$ kJ/kg

5. Energy Balance (Unsteady Flow): $W=0$ for rigid tank.

$$ Q_{cv} + \sum m_i h_i = (m_2 u_2 - m_1 u_1)_{cv} + \sum m_e h_e $$

Here, $m_e=0$.

$$ Q_{cv} + m_i h_i = m_2 u_2 - m_1 u_1 $$ $$ Q_{cv} = m_2 u_2 - m_1 u_1 - m_i h_i $$ $$ Q_{cv} = (23.229 \times 214.36) - (2.323 \times 214.36) - (20.906 \times 350.78) $$ $$ Q_{cv} = 4979.5 - 497.9 - 7333.0 $$ $$ Q_{cv} = 4481.6 - 7333.0 = -2851.4 \text{ kJ} \approx -2851 \text{ kJ} $$

(Negative sign indicates heat removed from the tank).

Table Values Used Summary: A.5 (R for Air), A.7.1 (u, h for Air).

Question 13: Ammonia Tank Filling Heat Transfer

Question:

A $1$ m³ tank initially contains ammonia (NH₃) at $150$ kPa, $25$°C. The tank is connected to a supply line flowing ammonia at $1200$ kPa, $60$°C. A valve is opened, allowing mass to flow into the tank until the tank is half full of liquid by volume ($V_f = 0.5$ m³) and the temperature inside is $25$°C. Calculate the heat transferred ($Q$) from the tank during this process.

Final Answer:

Heat Transfer $Q \approx -379$ MJ (Heat removed).

Full Solution:

1. System: Ammonia in the tank (CV). Unsteady filling.

2. States & Properties:

  • Initial (1): $P_1=150$ kPa, $T_1=25$°C. Superheated. Use interpolation in B.2.2 between 20°C and 30°C: $v_1 \approx 0.9895$ m³/kg, $u_1 \approx 1380.5$ kJ/kg.
  • Inlet (i): $P_i=1200$ kPa, $T_i=60$°C. Superheated. Use interpolation in B.2.2 between 1000 kPa and 1400 kPa: $h_i \approx 1551.15$ kJ/kg.
  • Final (2): $V=1$ m³, $T_2=25$°C. $V_f = 0.5$ m³. Saturated mixture. From B.2.1 at 25°C: $v_f=0.001658$, $v_g=0.12813$, $u_f=296.59$, $u_g=1335.0$.

3. Mass Calculations:

  • $m_1 = V / v_1 = 1 / 0.9895 = 1.0106$ kg.
  • Final masses:
    $m_{f2} = V_f / v_f = 0.5 / 0.001658 = 301.57$ kg.
    $m_{g2} = (V - V_f) / v_g = 0.5 / 0.12813 = 3.902$ kg.
  • $m_2 = m_{f2} + m_{g2} = 301.57 + 3.902 = 305.47$ kg.
  • Mass added: $m_i = m_2 - m_1 = 305.47 - 1.0106 = 304.46$ kg.

4. Calculate Final Internal Energy ($U_2$):

$$ U_2 = m_{f2}u_f + m_{g2}u_g = (301.57 \times 296.59) + (3.902 \times 1335.0) $$ $$ U_2 = 89480 + 5209.2 = 94689.2 \text{ kJ} $$

5. Calculate Initial Internal Energy ($U_1$):

$$ U_1 = m_1 u_1 = 1.0106 \times 1380.5 = 1395.1 \text{ kJ} $$

6. Energy Balance: $Q_{cv} + m_i h_i = U_2 - U_1$

$$ Q_{cv} = (U_2 - U_1) - m_i h_i $$ $$ Q_{cv} = (94689.2 - 1395.1) - (304.46 \times 1551.15) $$ $$ Q_{cv} = 93294.1 - 472251 = -378957 \text{ kJ} \approx -379 \text{ MJ} $$

(Negative sign indicates heat removed).

Question 14: Polytropic Ammonia Compression

Question:

A device processes $2$ kg of ammonia (NH₃) from an initial state of $150$ kPa, $-20$°C to a final state of $400$ kPa, $80$°C in a polytropic process ($Pv^n = \text{constant}$). Assuming the process is internally reversible, find:
a) The polytropic exponent $n$.
b) The work done ($W$).
c) The heat transfer ($Q$).
d) The total entropy generated, assuming heat transfer occurs with a source at $100$°C.

Final Answer:

a) $n \approx 1.905$
b) Work $W \approx -156.9$ kJ (Work input).
c) Heat Transfer $Q \approx 184.3$ kJ.
d) Total Entropy Generation $S_{gen} \approx 0.701$ kJ/K.

Full Solution:

1. Identify States:

  • State 1: $P_1=150$ kPa, $T_1=-20$°C. Superheated. From B.2.2: $v_1=0.7977$ m³/kg, $u_1=1303.3$ kJ/kg, $s_1=5.7465$ kJ/kg-K.
  • State 2: $P_2=400$ kPa, $T_2=80$°C. Superheated. From B.2.2: $v_2=0.47666$ m³/kg, $u_2=1473.9$ kJ/kg, $s_2=6.3437$ kJ/kg-K.
  • Mass $m = 2$ kg. Reservoir $T_H = 100$°C $= 373.15$ K.

2. Find Polytropic Exponent (n):

$P_1 v_1^n = P_2 v_2^n \implies \frac{P_2}{P_1} = (\frac{v_1}{v_2})^n \implies n = \frac{\ln(P_2/P_1)}{\ln(v_1/v_2)}$

$$ n = \frac{\ln(400/150)}{\ln(0.7977 / 0.47666)} = \frac{\ln(2.6667)}{\ln(1.6735)} = \frac{0.98085}{0.5149} \approx 1.9049 $$

3. Calculate Work (W): For internally reversible polytropic process ($n \neq 1$):

$$ W = \frac{m(P_2 v_2 - P_1 v_1)}{1 - n} $$ $$ W = \frac{2 \text{ kg} \times (400 \times 0.47666 - 150 \times 0.7977) \text{ kJ}}{1 - 1.9049} $$ $$ W = \frac{2 \times (190.664 - 119.655)}{-0.9049} = \frac{2 \times 71.009}{-0.9049} = \frac{142.018}{-0.9049} = -156.94 \text{ kJ} $$

(Work input $\approx 156.9$ kJ).

4. Calculate Heat Transfer (Q): First Law: $Q = \Delta U + W = m(u_2 - u_1) + W$.

$$ Q = 2 \text{ kg} \times (1473.9 - 1303.3) \frac{\text{kJ}}{\text{kg}} + (-156.94 \text{ kJ}) $$ $$ Q = 2 \times 170.6 - 156.94 = 341.2 - 156.94 = 184.26 \text{ kJ} \approx 184.3 \text{ kJ} $$

5. Calculate Entropy Generation ($S_{gen}$): $S_{gen} = \Delta S_{sys} + \Delta S_{surr}$.

  • $\Delta S_{sys} = m(s_2 - s_1) = 2 \text{ kg} \times (6.3437 - 5.7465) \frac{\text{kJ}}{\text{kg-K}} = 2 \times 0.5972 = 1.1944$ kJ/K
  • $\Delta S_{surr} = \frac{-Q}{T_H} = \frac{-184.26 \text{ kJ}}{373.15 \text{ K}} = -0.4938$ kJ/K
$$ S_{gen} = 1.1944 - 0.4938 = 0.7006 \frac{\text{kJ}}{\text{K}} \approx 0.701 \text{ kJ/K} $$

6. P-v Diagram: Draw saturation dome. Plot states 1 and 2 (both superheated). Draw curve $Pv^{1.905}=C$ connecting them.

Table Values Used Summary: B.2.2 (Ammonia).
